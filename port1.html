COMPUTER SCIENCE AS A DISCIPLINE

Computer science is a multifaceted field that encompasses scientific and engineering aspects, which are manifested in algorithmic problem-solving processes.

The discipline of computer science was born in the early 1940s with the confluence of algorithm theory, mathematical logic, and the invention of the stored-program electronic computer.

The discipline of computer science is the body of knowledge and practices used by computing professionals in their work.

It encompasses practical areas like software development, artificial intelligence, and cybersecurity, as well as theoretical subfields focusing on the mathematical principles behind computation.

FIVE COMPUTING DISCIPLINES AND MAJORS

COMPUTER ENGINEERING

Computer Engineering is focused on computer architecture and infrastructure, from the applied to the theoretical aspects. It also has an interest in software methods (programming) insofar as it applies to the hardware side of computing.

Computer Engineers are focused on the connection between hardware and software. A dominant area within computing engineering is embedded systems: the development of devices that have software and hardware embedded in them.

COMPUTER SCIENCE

Computer science has the widest range of computing topics. It focuses especially on the theoretical aspects of computing, leaving the more applied topics (and organizational and architecture issues) to other disciplines.

Computer Scientists develop a strong foundation based on mathematics and algorithms. They are trained to discover the best solutions for new problems, generate new technologies and come up with innovative cutting-edge ideas.

INFORMATION SYSTEMS

Information Systems is focused on the organizational issues of computing. Information Systems also has an interested in the applied aspects of application technologies and software development.

Information Systems specialists integrate information technology with business processes. They focus on the processing of information, and must have an understanding of how organizations and technology work.

INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY

Information Technology is focused on the applied side of computing. It covers all aspects of technology infrastructure, including hardware, operating systems, applications, data storage and communication systems.

Information Technology professionals provide customer service with a focus on technology. They work to meet the computer technology needs of business, government, healthcare, schools, and other kinds of organizations.

SOFTWARE ENGINEERING

Software Engineering is focused on everything (from applied to theoretical) related to software methods, that is, writing software. Infrastructure and application technologies are also part of software engineering.

Software Engineers develop and maintain large-scale software systems. Using the principles of mathematics and computer science, and the practices of engineering, software engineers learn how to develop software that meets costumer needs.

ANALYSIS/REACTION

There are 5 computing disciplines and majors, mainly computer science, computer engineering, software engineering, information technology, and information systems. Selecting the correct computing degree discipline is important if you want to look for an area of study and a career in technology and computers. Understanding these computing disciplines and the abilities you will learn from studying them would help you with your future career and choices. Each discipline offers a unique perspective and approach to problem-solving, catering to different interests, and skills.

Computer engineering is more on hardware than on software, but Computer Engineers use both for integrated devices. Computer Engineers apply engineering theories to the problems of designing computers and computer-based devices. Software Engineering focuses more on software reliability, and it focuses on the techniques for developing and maintaining appropriate software solutions. Information Technology is responsible for planning and managing the entire technology lifecycle; they integrate the software into the hardware. Information Systems studies the combination of business and computing coursework, and may have a computing focus or a management focus. Computer Science is the study of discovering the best solutions for new problems, generating new technologies, and coming up with innovative, cutting-edge ideas. The discipline of computer science shows the body of knowledge of computing and is frequently described as the systematic study of algorithmic processes that describe and transform information: their theory, analysis, design, efficiency, implementation, and application. Even though computer science addresses both human-made and natural information processes, the main effort in the discipline has been directed toward human-made processes, especially information processing systems and machines.

Understanding and analyzing these disciplines of computing would be a great help to you with your future career and choices. Each discipline offers a unique perspective and approach to problem-solving, catering to different interests and skills. Knowing the differences would give you more knowledge of the track you're into.
